Adopt a marine forest

Citizens, non-governmental organizations and private companies are encouraging conservation, understanding and appreciation of local marine environments by adopting a marine forest.

“Adoptive parents” are monitoring the conservation status of local marine forests, and organizing activities to raise awareness and appreciation of marine environments.

What to adopt a marine forest, send us an e-mail to marineforests@ualg.pt.

Species richness of kelp forests

Species richness of kelps is higher in the north-east Pacific (maximum 32 species) and lower at higher latitudes (e.g., Siberia and the Baltic Sea), where extensive ice coverage and low-salinity regimes prevail. Regions of high endemism are in the Galapagos Islands, Antarctica, South Africa and East Russia.
