Future biodiversity impacts

Global estimates of the impacts of climat change on the biodiversity of marine forests.

Intra-specific genetic diversity reflects speciesâ€™ evolutionary adaptations to new environmental conditions and therefore it is considered key for their resilience to ongoing and future climate change. Yet, global estimates of regions of high intraspecific diversity are still lacking, hindering efforts for conservation prioritization.

We examine the drivers shaping the genetic diversity and predicted diversity patterns for > 400 marine forest species.

We find a negative relationship between the genetic diversity and the environmental distance between niche centroid and Last Glacial Maximum (LGM) conditions, highlighting the role of climate stability in conferring higher levels of genetic diversity. We predict regions of high genetic diversity for multiple species along the coasts of Northeastern Pacific, Northeastern Atlantic and Southern Australia. The predicted patterns of higher genetic diversity match global patterns of species richness, stressing the role of climate stability as a common driver shaping marine forest biodiversity from genes to ecosystems.
