Diversity of Marine Forests

Global patterns of species diversity of macroalgae and seagrass forests.

Marine forests of macroalgae ans seagrass create essential habitats for coastal species and support invaluable ecological services. Project Marine Forests provides the first global analysis of species richness of kelp, fucoid and seagrass biomes.

A large dataset of macroalgal observations (aprox. 2 million records) where coupled with relevant environmental predictors (i.e., light, temperature, salinity, nitrate, wave energy and ice coverage) to develop stacked species distribution models (stacked SDMs) and yield estimates of global species richness and endemicity.

The mapped biodiversity patterns can serve as new baselines for planning and prioritizing locations for conservation, man-agement and climate change mitigation strategies, flagging threatened marine forest regions under different climate change scenarios.

Species richness of kelp forests

The dataset provides a fine-tuned dataset of marine ecosystem-structuring species of large brown algae and seagrasses with dereplicated occurrence records, taxonomically standardized, and considering important physiological and biogeographical traits. The dataset is documented in the peer reviewed article "Assis, J., Fragkopoulou, E., Frade, D. et al. A fine-tuned global distribution dataset of marine forests. Sci Data 7, 119 (2020). https://doi.org/10.1038/s41597-020-0459-x"
